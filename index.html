01. Problem Overview

The program is losing nearly half its inventory with no way to notice.

BorrowBox is a free gear lending service run by the City of Toronto across twelve neighbourhood hubs. Patrons borrow tools, sports equipment, and camping gear at no cost. But the program operates with no item-level tracking, only manual spreadsheets, and has no system to monitor items after they leave a hub.

Patrons Hub Staff City Administrators Operations Managers

The gap produces three compounding, measurable failures that directly affect every patron who walks into a hub expecting to borrow equipment.

23.3%
Non-Return Rate
Items That Never Come Back
Nearly 1 in 4 checked-out items is never returned. Camping equipment is worst at 30.7%. Without patron-linked item records, staff have no reliable mechanism to identify missing items or contact responsible patrons.
39.6%
Damage Rate on Return
Items Returned Damaged
Of items that do come back, 39.6% arrive damaged or with missing parts and cannot be re-lent until repaired. There is no condition logging system, so damage cannot be attributed to specific patrons.
63.3
Average Days Overdue
No Overdue Detection
Without tracking, overdue items remain undetected for an average of 63 days before staff notice. By then, recovery is often impossible. Annual replacement cost: approximately $95,000.

02. Data Design

Two synthetic datasets. One story of before and after.

Both datasets were generated using Python (pandas, numpy) with 2,520 records each, calibrated to documented BorrowBox operational statistics (Dataset 1) and Canadian library case study benchmarks (Dataset 2).

Dataset 1. Current State

2,520 records × current BorrowBox conditions

  • Non-return probability = Base_Rate(Category) × Hub_Multiplier × Month_Multiplier
  • Base rates: Tools 18.4%, Sports 21.2%, Camping 31.2%
  • Hub multipliers: 0.75 (best) → 1.35 (worst) reflecting hub-level variation
  • Seasonal multiplier: 0.80 in January rising to 1.19 in December
  • Return condition: Good/Damaged/Missing Parts/Unusable by category distribution
  • Replacement costs: Tools $45–$280, Sports $25–$180, Camping $60–$350
Dataset 2. Post-QR State

2,520 records × post-implementation benchmarks

  • Non-return rates reduced: Tools 4.2%, Sports 3.8%, Camping 5.5%
  • Return condition improved: Good 82–85% depending on category
  • Days overdue compressed to U(1,8), reflecting alert-driven rapid recovery
  • Alert entity added: 81.4% of overdue transactions show patron response within 48h
  • Calibrated to Canadian library QR tracking deployment benchmarks
  • Random seed: 99 (reproducible; seed: 42 for Dataset 1)
Data Model: 5 Entities
Item (QR_Code, Status, Category) Patron (ID, Email, Phone) Transaction (Checkout, Return, Condition) Alert (Sent_Date, Responded) Hub (Location, Capacity)

04. Proposed Solution

A QR scan at every checkout and return. That's the whole intervention.

The BorrowBox QR Inventory Tracking System attaches a unique QR label to every item. Every transaction creates a complete, patron-linked record. Overdue alerts dispatch automatically within 24 hours. Return condition is logged at scan. Nothing requires new hardware beyond a tablet or phone camera.

01 📋
Checkout
Staff scan QR + enter patron ID. System creates transaction, sets due date, status → On Loan.

06. Conclusion & Impact

A $0 software cost that saves $77,000 a year.

Primary Outcome

Inventory availability rises from 46.3% to 76.7%, a 30 percentage point gain. More items available means more patrons served, which is the entire point of the program.

Financial Impact

Annual loss cost falls from $95,000 to $18,000. an 81% reduction. QR label cost per item: under $0.10. Full system cost: $0 in software licensing. ROI is measured in weeks.

Scope Pivot

Original scope: no-show prediction ML model (AUC 0.82. target met). Redirected by Dr. Cardona in Week 6 to the more significant inventory loss problem. ML model retained as secondary deliverable.

Academic Contribution

16 verified sources. Synthetic datasets calibrated to Canadian library benchmarks. TOGAF ADM enterprise architecture. 6 KPIs with formulas derived from dataset variables. All validated against dashboard outputs.
